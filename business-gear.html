My Filmmaking Gear

Here’s a breakdown of the tools and equipment that help bring each story to life.

Cameras & Lenses

  • Sony FX3: My main camera for cinematic quality and versatility.
  • Sony A7S III: Perfect for low light and fast-paced events.
  • 24-70mm f/2.8 G Master: The all-around workhorse lens.
  • 70-200mm f/2.8 G Master: For sports, ceremonies, and distance shots.

Audio

  • Rode Wireless GO II: Compact, reliable, and perfect for interviews.
  • Zoom H6 Recorder: My go-to for clear, professional-grade sound.

Stabilization

  • DJI RS 3 Pro: Smooth gimbal movement for dynamic shots.
  • Manfrotto Tripod: Stable and reliable for interviews and locked shots.

Lighting

  • Aputure 120d II: Bright and soft key light for interviews.
  • Godox LED Panels: Great for mobile lighting setups.

Editing

  • MacBook Pro M3 Max: Handles 4K+ footage with ease.
  • DaVinci Resolve Studio: My main color grading and editing software.
  • Adobe Premiere Pro: Used for specific edits and collaborative projects.

Each piece of gear plays a role in creating professional, emotionally driven films that tell your story the right way.
